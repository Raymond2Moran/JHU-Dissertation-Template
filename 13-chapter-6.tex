\chapter{Conclusion and Discussion} \label{chap:conclusion}

\section{Conclusion}

This thesis developed and implemented a full analysis workflow that integrates protein edge prediction with prediction-powered inference and sample-size planning. By combining pretrained biological representations with calibration-budget-aware estimators, the framework supports both practical model comparison and statistically valid uncertainty reporting.

\section{Key Findings}

The empirical and inferential results support four major conclusions:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Representation choice materially impacts both predictive accuracy and downstream uncertainty efficiency.
    \item Adding text-derived features can improve robustness, but gains are data- and split-dependent.
    \item PPI and PPI++ provide useful uncertainty control under constrained labeled budgets, with PPI++ often showing efficiency advantages.
    \item The same inference principles transfer to non-biological AI-evaluation contexts (LLM-as-a-judge), suggesting broad applicability.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Limitations}

Several limitations remain:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item External dataset preparation depends on mapping quality and negative-edge construction assumptions.
    \item Confidence-interval behavior for derived nonlinear metrics may be sensitive in low-prevalence settings.
    \item High-capacity embedding models increase compute cost and can constrain repeated calibration experiments.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Future Work}

Future directions include:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Extending inference targets to additional network-level functionals.
    \item Improving external benchmark harmonization across curated PPI resources.
    \item Exploring adaptive labeling strategies guided by prediction-powered variance diagnostics.
    \item Integrating biologically informed priors from structured knowledge bases and literature-guided models.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Final Remarks}

A central practical outcome of this work is reproducibility: all major analyses are script-driven, unified by a single notebook interface, and directly connected to thesis-ready tables and figures. This workflow is intended to be reusable for subsequent biological studies where labeled data are limited but predictive surrogates are available at scale.
